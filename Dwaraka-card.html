⇒Dwarka Temple, also known as Dwarkadhish Temple, is one of the four sacred Char Dham pilgrimage sites in India. It is located in Dwarka city, Gujarat, and is dedicated to Lord Krishna, who is worshipped here as the "King of Dwarka." The temple is believed to be over 2,500 years old, though the current structure was rebuilt in the 16th century. It stands on the banks of the Gomti River and features a grand five-storied structure made of limestone. The temple's spire rises to about 78 meters and is adorned with a huge flag that changes daily. Dwarka is considered the ancient kingdom of Lord Krishna and holds immense religious importance for Hindus.
